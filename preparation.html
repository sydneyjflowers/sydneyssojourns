P r e p a r a t i o n

Save the stress and prepare properly!

Planning 101

  1. Research Your Destination:
    • Start by thoroughly researching your destination. Learn about its culture, customs, language, laws, and major attractions. Understanding the place you're visiting will enhance your trip greatly.
  2. Create a Travel Itinerary:
    • Plan your trip schedule, including the places you want to visit, activities you want to do, and a rough agenda. Make sure to make reservations early if necessary. Having a plan can help you make the most of your time and ensure you don't miss out on must-see attractions.
  3. Budget:
    • Determine a travel budget that includes transportation, housing, food, activities, and emergency funds. Stick to your budget to avoid overspending and financial stress during your trip. This is especially important as a college student!
  4. Pack Wisely:
    • Pack according to the weather and activities you have planned. Make a packing list to ensure you don't forget essential items like travel documents, medications, technological devices, and travel adapters if necessary. Reference the master packing list below!
  5. Travel Documents and Health Precautions:
    • Make sure you have all necessary travel documents such as passports, visas, and ID cards. Also, check if you need new vaccines or other health precautions like travel insurance. Make copies of important documents and store them separately and safely.

Master Packing List

  1. Travel Documents:
    • Passport and visa (if required)
    • Driver's license or ID card
    • Travel insurance documents
    • Itinerary and reservations
    • Vaccination certificates (if needed, make sure to research your destination's requirements!)
    • Emergency contact information
    • Copies of important documents
    • Money and Payment:
    • Cash (both local and your home currency)
    • Credit/debit cards
    • Travel wallet
  2. Health and Medications:
    • Prescription medications
    • Over-the-counter medications (pain relievers, antacids, etc.)
    • First-aid kit (band-aids, antiseptic wipes, etc.)
    • Personal hygiene items (toothbrush, toothpaste, etc.)
    • Prescription eyewear and contacts, if applicable
  3. Clothing: Make sure to pack based on activities/weather!
    • Underwear and socks
    • Shirts/tops
    • Pants/shorts
    • Dresses/skirts
    • Sweaters/jackets
    • Rain jacket or poncho
    • Swimwear
    • Sleepwear
    • Comfortable walking shoes
    • Dress shoes (if needed)
    • Sandals/flip-flops
    • Hat or cap
    • Belts
  4. Accessories:
    • Sunglasses
    • Jewelry
    • Scarves or neckties
    • Umbrella
  5. Gear:
    • Luggage (suitcase, backpack, or duffel bag)
    • Daypack or small bag for daily outings
    • Travel pillow
    • Earplugs and eye mask
    • TSA-approved locks
    • Luggage tags
  6. Toiletries:
    • Shampoo and conditioner
    • Body wash or soap
    • Razor and shaving cream
    • Hairbrush/comb
    • Hairdryer (if necessary)
    • Deodorant
    • Feminine hygiene products
    • Contact lens solution and case
    • Nail clippers
  7. Miscellaneous:
    • Travel guidebook/maps
    • Notepad and pen
    • Entertainment (books, e-reader, playing cards)
    • Reusable water bottle
    • Snacks
    • Ziplock bags (for organizing and storing items)
    • Travel laundry detergent (for longer trips)
    • Stain remover
    • Bug spray
  8. Electronics:
    • Phone and charger
    • Laptop/tablet and charger
    • Camera and accessories
    • Power bank and charging cables
    • Travel adapter and voltage converter (if needed)
